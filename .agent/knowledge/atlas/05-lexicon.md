## Fragments & Vectors

Okay so here's the way I see the fragments. There are 4 fragments, temporal in nature and these are ETERNAL, PAST, PRESENT and FUTURE. Of those fragments ETERNAL and PRESENT are then further divided into PHYSICAL and NON-PHYSICAL/MENTAL. The PAST and FUTURE fragments contain "log entries" or whatever in the form of VECTORS (user facing past vectors are called memories but they function the same).

This means that a vector is NOT a fragment. However, the fragment is the sum of all vectors relating to that fragment.
Eternal.physical is NOT a fragment. However together with Eternal.Non_physical it is.
